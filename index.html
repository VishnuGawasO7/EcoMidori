Common Questions

Quick answers to help you get started with circular entrepreneurship

What makes a business truly circular vs just "green"?

While "green" businesses might use recycled materials, truly circular ventures design out waste entirely. They create systems where materials continuously circulate at their highest value, products are designed for disassembly and reuse, and business models prioritize access over ownership.

How can startups access funding for circular projects?

Circular ventures can tap into climate tech funds, impact investment networks, corporate sustainability partnerships, and government grants specifically for circular innovation. We help connect founders with our network of 50+ impact investors actively seeking circular business opportunities.

Is circular entrepreneurship actually profitable?

Absolutely. Circular models often reduce material costs by 30-50%, create new revenue streams from waste valorization, and build stronger customer relationships through service-based models. The average circular venture in our portfolio achieves profitability 40% faster than traditional startups.

What's the first step to start a circular business?

Begin with a material flow analysis of your product or service. Identify where value is being lost through waste, then explore circular strategies that recover that value. Our Circular Founder's Playbook provides a step-by-step framework to guide you through this process.
