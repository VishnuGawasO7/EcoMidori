Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about implementing circular economy principles

What's the difference between recycling and circular economy?

Recycling is just one component of circular economy. True circularity involves designing out waste from the beginning, keeping products and materials in use longer, and regenerating natural systems, not just managing waste at the end of life.

How can small businesses afford circular transformation?

Start with material flow mapping to identify "low-hanging fruit" - opportunities that reduce costs while improving circularity. Many circular strategies actually reduce expenses through material efficiency and can be implemented gradually.

Is circular economy only for manufacturing companies?

No, circular principles apply to all sectors. Service businesses can adopt circular procurement, reduce resource use in operations, and design services that enable circularity for their clients.

How do we measure circular economy success?

Key metrics include material circularity rate, product lifetime extension, percentage of renewable/recycled inputs, waste reduction, and financial benefits from circular activities like material cost savings and new revenue streams.
