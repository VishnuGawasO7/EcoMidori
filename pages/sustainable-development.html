The 17 Sustainable Development Goals

A universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future.

The 17 SDGs are integratedâ€”they recognize that action in one area will affect outcomes in others, and that development must balance social, economic and environmental sustainability.

Key Principles:

  • Universality: The goals apply to all countries, not just developing nations
  • Integration: The goals are interconnected and cannot be achieved in isolation
  • Leave No One Behind: Prioritizing progress for those furthest behind first
  • Inclusiveness: Engaging all stakeholders in implementation

Implementing the SDGs

How countries and organizations are working towards the 2030 goals

The SDGs are implemented through a combination of government action, private sector engagement, and civil society participation. Each country is responsible for implementing the goals according to their national contexts and priorities.

Progress and Challenges

While progress has been made on several goals, the world is not on track to achieve most of the SDGs by 2030. The COVID-19 pandemic has reversed some of the progress made, and many regions face significant challenges.

Progress Made

  • Extreme poverty has been reduced significantly since 1990
  • Child mortality rates have declined globally
  • Access to electricity has increased in many regions

Key Challenges

  • Rising inequalities within and between countries
  • Climate change impacts accelerating
  • Conflict and fragility disrupting development
